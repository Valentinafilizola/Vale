Project Brief

Skills

This project strengthened my ability to bridge design and engineering workflows while applying user-centered design principles.

Background

Eat Together is a student-led startup redefining campus dining by turning meals into meaningful social experiences. The mobile app connects students through food meetups, making it easier to build friendships beyond the classroom.

With over 400 students already on board, the platform is constantly evolving, enhancing features, and finding new ways to bring people together.

Initial Research: Competitive Analysis

Competitive analysis helped identify proven interaction patterns for onboarding, discovery, personalization, and community-building that could translate into stronger profile features for students.

MeetUp

MeetUp was selected for competitive analysis because of its success in fostering social connections through seamless event organization and user engagement. Its intuitive design, effective onboarding, and community-driven approach provided key insights for enhancing Eat Together’s platform—helping refine how students discover connections and personalize their profile effortlessly.

StoryGraph

The StoryGraph app was selected for competitive analysis because of its strong focus on personalized user experiences. Its data-driven recommendations, customizable user profiles, and intuitive tagging system provided valuable insights for Eat Together’s profile features. This research helped us design a more tailored and engaging way for students to showcase their dining preferences and connect over shared interests.

Value Proposition

What is our product, who is it for, and how does it drive user value?

Our redesigned mobile app helps college students who want to connect through shared meals by providing an intuitive, mobile-first experience to reduce friction around planning and increase real-world connection.

Design Guide

I joined the Eat Together project with an existing design guide, so I made sure my design iterations respected the established colors, typography, and components. I focused on maintaining a cohesive look and feel by aligning my updates with the original design system. This approach helped preserve the app's familiar and user-friendly experience while enhancing its usability.

The Solution

The redesigned Eat Together profile page enhances the user experience with a clean and intuitive layout. Key features include an improved settings page, a section to view your connections even when you don’t have any yet, customizable tags to showcase your interests, and a restaurant saver for keeping track of favorite spots.

Reflecting on Change

Our solution is better than the current Eat Together profile page because it introduces key features that enhance usability and personalization. The updated design includes an improved settings page, a way to view your connections even if you have none yet, customizable tags, and a restaurant saver—making it easier for users to connect and manage their profiles effortlessly.

The updated design ensures a user-friendly experience for everyone, with clear navigation, responsive layouts for different devices, and an improved visual hierarchy. These enhancements make the profile page more accessible, personalized, and aligned with the app’s goal of fostering connections through shared interests and favorite spots.

What I Learned

Redesigning the Eat Together profile page taught me how to build clear information architecture and balance simplicity with engaging design. I also developed project management skills by coordinating feedback, setting timelines, and ensuring that design updates aligned with existing guidelines. This experience helped me adapt designs effectively while enhancing how users connect through shared interests and favorite spots.

What Could Be Better

More user testing early on would have uncovered additional insights. Future iterations should focus on scaling new features smoothly and maintaining a consistent experience across different devices, while effective project management can help prioritize these improvements.

Future Opportunities

As we prepare to test the feasibility of the designs and move towards App Store development, I’ll be conducting more usability testing to ensure that the features are practical, scalable, and truly enhance how users connect through shared interests.
